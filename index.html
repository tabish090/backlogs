The Backlog Blues

Oh, backlog, beast of towering files,

A mountain built of stress and sighs.

Deadlines loom, a looming storm,

While emails pile, a digital swarm.

Tasks unfinished, a tangled web,

Where productivity met its bed.

Meetings drone, decisions stall,

Motivation takes a bitter fall.

The inbox groans, a hungry beast,

Each ping a taunt, a mental feast.

The calendar, a battlefield,

Where minutes flee, with no yield.

But wait, dear friend, there's hope within,

To slay the beast and start to win.

Prioritize, a warrior's tool,

To break the chains and make it cool.

Chunk it down, a bite at a time,

Conquer tasks, and watch them climb.

Delegate, a magic spell,

Share the burden, break the shell.

Automate, a loyal friend,

Repetitive tasks, till the very end.

Celebrate each victory won,

No matter how small, the battle's begun.

So rise above, the backlog's call,

You've got the strength, to stand up tall.

With focus, grit, and mindful grace,

Turn that backlog, into a happy space.

Remember, friend, you're not alone,

In this battle for the task-free zone.

We'll conquer mountains, file by file,

And leave the backlog, with a smile.
